ABOUT CEC

Since 1965 Campus Events Commission (CEC) has been one of the largest purveyors of campus enter tainment. From its inception, it has acquired a reputation for its edgy style of advertising and controversial events. CEC has been responsible for hosting the Jack Benny and Spencer Tracy Award shows, and for holding the renowned Shorttakes Student Film Festival.

Still pushing the envelope today, CEC has remained a student-run, student-funded organization, dedicated to introducing the freshest fare in film, music and speakers into the UCLA community. From hosting weekly free-$ movies to unabashedly showcasing free sneaks of films before they hit theatres, CEC has become the students' premiere source for new cinema. CEC also brings the best in music programming to students by providing free quarterly concerts in various venues throughout the UCLA campus, as well as booking bands and artists to perform at the annual Bruin Bash.

In addition to providing new film and music, CEC coordinates speaking events from an eclectic variety of dignitaries. In the past, CEC has been responsible for obtaining speakers that range from Martin Luther King Jr. to Morgan Freeman and Michael Moore. Be it media seen or heard, Campus Events Commission is constantly in search of that which is as evocative and diverse as its audience.

PRINT MARKETING

Campus Events Commission's Print Marketing is a compilation of student designers from the UCLA campus. Print marketing creates every identity for each concert, film, and speakers event, as well as various CEC brand material. Not only limited to graphic design, Print Marketing strives to explore new mediums of advertisement through video and collaborations with web. This collective approaches graphic design and advertisement as an opportunity to build off of creativity, and continues to maintain its position as the leading student advertising group on campus. Members from the Print Marketing staff have also gained recognition for their designs from publicized design communities. This staff works with General Marketing to get the word out about events in designing film calendars found every week on campus, and all of the print graphics. Like capitalism, without advertising Campus Events would crumble.

ONLINE MARKETING

Campus Events Commission boasts an extensive online presence, which has been continually expanded and carefully maintained by the Online Marketing Team. Event information is dispersed throughout campus with daily updates to the Commission's personal Facebook and Twitter pages, which reach in excess of 4000 students combined. Online Marketing also designs and distributes a weekly newsletter that highlights upcoming CEC events and special offers for a readership of over 6500 students. Online Marketing staff utilizes all of these mediums to give interested students the information they need in a medium they prefer.

The primary hub of CEC's publicity efforts focuses on Online Marketing's self designed, central web site. This information nexus houses not only event information, but a general interest blog maintained by all the staffs and a deeply extensive event archive spanning back to 1977. Online Marketing keeps the page always up to date, rapidly responding to changes as they occur. Online Marketing also uses the site to manage the Audience Appreciation program, which rewards frequent attendees for their continued support. Using these combined methods of web marketing and information distribution, Online Marketing forms the primary mode of contact between the Commission and the student body we serve.

CONCERTS

FILM FESTIVALS

The CEC Festivals Department works to put on UCLA's two biggest film festivals, Campus MovieFest and the Shorttakes Student Film Festival.

Campus MovieFest - campusmoviefest.com

Campus MovieFest is nationwide campus-touring program that provides students all they need to create a film within a week. CEC works with Campus MovieFest to rent and staff campus venues, and direct online, in-person, and creative marketing. Due to CEC's extensive and thorough audience reach, UCLA now puts on Campus MovieFest's largest local festival, receiving over 100 sign-ups and over 300 attendees, thanks to CEC.

Shorttakes Student Film Festival - cecshorttakes.com

Shorttakes is UCLA's only film festival created, directed, and produced entirely by UCLA undergrads. Drawing on the ingenuity of a small but dedicated team, Shorttakes recently went green by switching to online submissions and judging. The Shorttakes Film Festival has had many esteemed judges in the past, such as:

  • Andrew J. Cohen (Producer; 40-Year-Old Virgin, Talladega Nights, Funny People)
  • Steven Banks (Writer; Spongebob Squarepants, CatDog)
  • Paul Schrader (Writer/director; Taxi Driver, Blue Collar, Raging Bull, Affliction)
  • Norman Buckley (Director/editor; Gossip Girl, Chuck, Melrose Place, 90210, Greek, The O.C.)
  • Gregory Poirier (Writer; Lion King 2, Tomcats, National Treasure 2)
  • Randy Cartwright (Writer/animator; Pocahontas, Hercules, Aladdin, Princess and the Frog)
  • Nancy Cartwright (Voicework; Bart Simpson and Ralph in The Simpsons)

In the past, Shorttakes has been sponsored companies such as Flip Video, Kodak Motion Picture Film, Entertainment Weekly, Filter Magazine, Rotten Tomatoes, Campus Circle, Indie 103.1, Underground Film, and Giant Robot. Many past Shorttakes Film Festival winners have gone onto later fame, such as ShaneAcker, the creator and director of the animated feature film 9.
